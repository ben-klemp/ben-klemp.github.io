Myth vs Legend

It is human nature to quesiton and search for answers about life, death, and the events that impact daily living. Often a myth offers an explanation - be it sensible or preposterous.

Myth:

Comes from an ancient culture

The characters are humans, animals, gods, or mythical creatures

Set an example of human behavior

Often talks about the creation of something

Passed down from generations

Unit 2

Types of Sentences

  • Simple Sentence: She breathed. One independent clause.
  • Compound Sentence: She took in one long breath, yet she still didn't feel completely at ease. Two or more independent clauses.
  • Complex Sentence: If your chest expands to much, you may not be breathing correctly. One independent and one subordinate clause.
  • Compound-Complex Sentence: Three sisters took yoga so that they could learn relaxation techniques, but their brother was not interested. Two or more independent clauses and at least one subordinate clause.

Types of Subordinate Clauses

  • Noun - acts as a noun and usually starts with that, what, whatever, who, whom, whoever, whomever. Whatever we study incresases our knowledge
  • Adverb - usually modifies a verb. Call me when you need my help.
  • Adjective - modifies a noun in the sentence and placed right next to the word it modifies. I saw a child who was crying.
  • Essential without commas. Nonessential with commas.

Paragraph Reminders

  • The Topic Sentence is the main idea of the paragraph.
  • Elaboration and development comes from facts, statistics, quotations, definitions, anecdotes, examples, reasons, and comparisons.
  • Coherent writing is logically organized so the reader can follow.
  • Conclude your paragraph with a restatement of the main idea.

5 Ways to Organize Your Writing

  1. Chronological - in order of time. Works well with narratices, expository writing about historical events, and steps in a process.
  2. Spatial - describe details in an orderly way. Left to right, near to far, or top to bottom. Used in descriptive writing.
  3. Order of Importance - used when trying to persuade your audience. Start with least important and end with most important - or the reverse.
  4. Logical - helps the reader understand the key points in the order that is necessary.
  5. Specific to General/General to Specific - build towards and support a general idea or pare down a general idea to focus on a more specific thought.

Persuasion Writing

  • Begin with a topic sentence that grabs the reader's atttentions.
  • Include an opinion statement that clearly expresses your point of view.
  • Present reasons and other evidence to support your opinion.
  • Arrange the supporting details in order of importance from most to least, or the reverse.
  • Include a call to action. This is a statement that says what the reader should do.
